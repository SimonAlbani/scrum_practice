Habitat

Red Pandas live within the forests of the Himalayas. The Himalayas are within Nepal and China. They like forests where the teperature stays between 50 - 77 degrees Fahrenheit. Sounds nice!

Mating and Reproduction

Red Pandas are mammals, meaning that the females birth live young.  Red Pandas mate in early winter and will sometimes mate with more than one partner.  Female Red Pandas carry their babies for about four months before giving birth.  Baby red pandas are born in an average litter size of one to four babies and are born in the late spring and summer. They build a nest or use hollow tree trunks or small caves to give birth to their newborn.

Behavior

Red Pandas are territorial and solitary. That means they like to have lots of time alone! They only like finding other pandas when they want to have babies. They are quiet creatures that like to sleep all day in the trees and play at night and dusk. Red pandas clean their furs like cats. They also don't like to be too hot or too cold and spend lots of time playing in their forest.
